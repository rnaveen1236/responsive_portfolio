Exoskeleton :

Presentation by Rishikumar (Exoskeleton)

An exoskeleton is a wearable external frame or suit that maybe powered

by various modes such as pneumatic, hydraulic, electric motors, etc.

  • Exoskeletons are wearable external frames that enhance the performance of military personnel by supporting and augmenting their strength and endurance in harsh conditions.
  • Types of Exoskeletons:
    1. Lower Limb Exoskeletons: Support or replace leg functions.
    1. Upper Limb Exoskeletons: Enhance or restore arm and hand functions.
    1. Full-Body Exoskeletons: Provide comprehensive support for the entire body.
    1. Military and Industrial Exoskeletons: Designed to improve strength, endurance, and agility for soldiers and workers.
  • Current Exoskeleton Drawbacks:
    • Existing models are battery or computer-powered.
    • Example: The current prototype can walk only 1.6 km on a full charge and lift 92 kg, with a walking speed of 0.76 m/s.
  • Proposed Exoskeleton Concept:
    • Operates based on Pascal's Law in fluid mechanics: A pressure change in a confined incompressible fluid is transmitted uniformly throughout the system.
    • The exoskeleton enhances lifting capabilities by relying on human strength and Pascal's Law.

Day - 5

Explain by Manikandan

To get started with Fusion 360, you first need to install the software by downloading it from the Autodesk website.

After signing up for an Autodesk account, you can choose the version that fits your needs, such as a student or trial version, and follow the prompts to install the program.

Once installed, you can create a new design by starting with a 2D sketch, then use powerful tools like Extrude, Revolve, and Loft to transform your design into 3D.

For more complex designs, Fusion 360 offers features like Assemblies and Simulation to test the functionality and performance of your design under real-world conditions.

Additionally, you can create and modify 2D sketches from images, making it easy to trace outlines and prepare designs for manufacturing, such as laser cutting.

For sheet metal projects, the FlatFAB tool within Fusion 360 helps create flat patterns from 3D models, simplifying the fabrication process.

Once your design is finalized, you can export it as a DXF or SVG file for laser cutting, and even customize cutting parameters like speed and material thickness.

This integration of design, testing, and manufacturing in one platform makes Fusion 360 an efficient and versatile tool for creating and producing designs.
